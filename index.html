cynaxis

cynaxis is an informal Cypriot initiative with a focus on improving civic access to publicly- and privately-held information. We hold that every person should have unencumbered and equal access to the information that shapes their life and their community, free of antipopular bias. cynaxis has its roots in openpatata. openpatata sprang in August 2015 of a desire to shed light on the business of the Cypriot parliamentâ€”what would've been a daunting task for the uninitiated. openpatata was a parliament monitor in the likeness of openparliament.ca and TheyVoteForYou (Australia). It preceded neavouli, a crowdsourced candidate database, and continuing inquiry into open data in Cyprus. For more on what we've accomplished so far, see below.

Work

  • openpatata-data

    Machine-readable parliament data

    The data are harvested from plenary transcript PDFs and the official parliament website and are made available as a data package in the Popolo standard. They include plenary sittings, questions and MPs.

  • someone has to do this: Discourse analysis around Open Data in Cyprus

    A research project taking off from discourse analysis around open data in Cyprus.

    The work was initially presented as part of NeMe.org's nEUROsis (Limassol, November 2016). It uses local case studies to critically examine the notion of the state as a hoarder of information that is impeding economic development.

  • neavouli

    Platform for crowdsourcing candidate data

    neavouli was created in the run-up to the 2016 legislative election. It contains volunteer-contributed information on all candidates in the election. Data is available for download in the CSV format and through a JSON API.

  • erotiseis

    MP question web interface

    erotiseis presents questions MPs posed to the executive in a searchable format and links to answers where available. Visualisations are in the pipeline.

  • foi

    Freedom of Information request platform

    A platform through which people will be able to request information from local and national government bodies. Questions and answers are logged and are made publicly available.

Our software is free and open source. Other works are released under a Creative Commons (CC) licence.
